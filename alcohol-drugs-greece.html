Alcohol & Drug Laws in Greece

Important legal and safety guidance for students during the Greece International Work Experience trip.

⚠️ Zero Tolerance for Illegal Drugs

Greece has strict laws regarding illegal drugs. Possession, use, or distribution of controlled substances can result in severe penalties including fines, arrest, and imprisonment. Students must follow all local laws during their stay.

Alcohol Laws in Greece

Alcohol is widely available in Greece and is commonly consumed in restaurants, bars, and social settings. However, students must drink responsibly and be aware of the legal and safety risks associated with excessive alcohol consumption.

The legal drinking age in Greece is generally recognised as 18 years old. Even though enforcement may vary in some tourist areas, students should follow the same responsible behaviour expected in the UK.

Alcohol consumption can impair judgement, increase risk-taking behaviour, and make individuals more vulnerable to accidents or crime. Students should always remain aware of their surroundings and avoid situations where alcohol may lead to unsafe decisions.

Drink Driving Laws

Greece has strict drink-driving laws and lower alcohol limits compared to some other countries. Police frequently carry out roadside checks, especially in tourist areas and during busy seasons.

Drivers found exceeding legal alcohol limits may face heavy fines, licence suspension, or arrest. For this reason, students should never attempt to drive after consuming alcohol.

  • Lower drink-driving limits than the UK
  • Regular police roadside checks
  • Heavy fines and legal penalties
  • Possible vehicle confiscation

Illegal Drugs

Possession or use of illegal drugs in Greece is a criminal offence. This includes substances such as cannabis, cocaine, ecstasy, and other controlled drugs.

Even small amounts can lead to arrest and legal consequences. Greek authorities treat drug offences seriously, and penalties may include fines, detention, or court proceedings.

Students should never accept substances from strangers or assume that laws are more relaxed in tourist areas. What may seem like a casual situation can quickly become a serious legal issue.

Risks for Tourists

Tourists may sometimes feel more relaxed about local rules while travelling, but it is important to remember that the same laws apply to visitors as they do to residents.

Alcohol or drug use can increase the risk of:

  • Accidents and injuries
  • Getting lost or separated from friends
  • Becoming a target for theft or scams
  • Legal problems with local authorities

Remaining responsible and aware helps ensure the trip remains safe and enjoyable for everyone.

Advice for Students

Students participating in the Greece International Work Experience programme should prioritise safety and follow the guidance provided by college staff and trip organisers.

  • Drink responsibly and know your limits
  • Never leave drinks unattended
  • Stay with friends when going out
  • Follow all local laws and regulations
  • Avoid any involvement with illegal drugs

Programme Expectations

Students representing the college while travelling abroad are expected to maintain professional behaviour at all times. This includes respecting local laws, demonstrating responsible conduct, and ensuring personal safety.

Failure to follow safety guidance or local laws may result in disciplinary action and could affect participation in the programme.
