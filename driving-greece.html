Key Driving Laws in Greece

Driving in Greece can be very different from driving in the UK. Road conditions, traffic behaviour, and enforcement rules may vary, so it is important that students understand the key regulations before renting or using any vehicles.

Drink Driving Laws

Greece has stricter drink-driving limits compared to the United Kingdom. Police frequently carry out roadside checks and penalties can include heavy fines, licence suspension, and vehicle confiscation. Students should never attempt to drive after consuming alcohol.

Speed Limits

Speed limits in Greece generally follow these guidelines:

  • 50 km/h in urban areas and towns
  • 90 km/h on rural roads
  • 110 km/h on expressways
  • 130 km/h on motorways

Always check road signs as speed limits may change depending on road conditions.

Motorcycles and Quad Bikes

Scooters, mopeds and quad bikes are common tourist rentals in Greece. However, they are often involved in accidents involving visitors who are unfamiliar with the roads. Helmets are mandatory for drivers and passengers. Some travel insurance policies may not cover accidents involving quad bikes or high-powered scooters.

Insurance and Documentation

If hiring a vehicle, ensure the rental company provides valid insurance coverage. Drivers must carry identification and driving licences at all times. Students should check whether an International Driving Permit is required.
