Passport Safety

Guidance on protecting your passport and travel documents during the Greece work experience trip.

⚠️ Your Passport Is Your Most Important Travel Document

Losing your passport abroad can cause serious travel disruption. Students should take extra care to protect their passport and keep it stored securely throughout the trip.

Why Passport Safety Matters

Your passport is the most important document you will carry when travelling internationally. It proves your identity and nationality and is required for entering and leaving countries.

If a passport is lost or stolen, it can take time and effort to obtain an emergency replacement. This may involve contacting the British Embassy, providing identification documents, and paying additional fees.

For this reason, students should always ensure their passport is stored securely and handled carefully during the trip.

Keeping Your Passport Safe

When travelling in Greece, it is important to avoid carrying your passport everywhere unless necessary. Many travellers choose to store their passport securely in their accommodation and carry a photocopy instead.

Students should follow these safety practices to reduce the risk of losing their passport:

  • Keep your passport in a secure place such as a hotel safe
  • Do not leave your passport unattended in public places
  • Avoid carrying it unnecessarily during daily activities
  • Keep a photocopy or digital copy of your passport
  • Store copies of important travel documents separately

Carrying Identification

While it is not always necessary to carry your passport when exploring, students should still carry some form of identification.

A photocopy of your passport or another form of ID can be useful if requested by authorities or needed for verification when entering certain locations or services.

What to Do If Your Passport Is Lost or Stolen

If your passport is lost or stolen while in Greece, it is important to take action immediately.

  • Report the loss or theft to local police
  • Inform your trip organiser or college staff
  • Contact the British Embassy or Consulate
  • Apply for an emergency travel document if required

Having copies of your passport details will help speed up this process.

British Embassy Support

The British Embassy can assist UK citizens who lose their passport abroad. They may issue an Emergency Travel Document which allows you to travel back to the UK or continue your journey.

However, this process can take time and may require identity verification and documentation, so it is always better to prevent loss by keeping your passport safe.

Advice for Students

Students participating in the Greece International Work Experience programme should take responsibility for safeguarding their travel documents at all times.

  • Keep passports secure in accommodation
  • Avoid carrying them unnecessarily
  • Keep photocopies or digital backups
  • Report any loss immediately
  • Follow guidance from staff and organisers

Taking these precautions will help ensure a smooth and stress-free travel experience for everyone involved.
